REIMAGINING SUSTAINABLE SHOPPING

At CVS Health, we believe sustainable shopping is healthy shopping.

Across 12 weeks, my goal was to figure out how to inspire customers to make more sustainable decisions and encourage wellness in surrounding communities.

The Research

Extensive secondary research and interviews illustrated many of the challenges shoppers have with buying sustainable.

- 30 Survey Participants
- 12 In-Depth User Interviews
- 10 Usability Tests
- 12 Work Weeks

The Challenge

THE DESIGN

Our key insights led us to 3 main design criteria:

Reframed Question:

How can we... create a friendly and easy-to-use system that incentivizes sustainable products for personal and environmental gain each time a customer shops in-store?

THE BUILD

To achieve our 3 design goals to educate, incentivize, and show direct impact, we implemented 5 in-app features.

THE BUILD

1. Incentivize: Using a gamified rewards system, allowing customers to earn rewards for buying sustainably and to see their direct impact with their virtual tree.

THE BUILD

2. Show Impact: Personalized metrics to reflect relatable aspects of customers' lives. Here, customers can see their direct impact to the environment from their purchases.

THE BUILD

3. Educate: Sustainability initiatives for customers to learn about their products lifecycle and how to dispose of them. We wanted to educate on the entire product lifecycle, not just when a customer reaches for a product on the shelf.

4. The 'sustainability promise' page presents customers with transparency and the concrete steps CVS is taking to uphold a sustainable customer experience.

THE BUILD

5. Finally, we implemented a digital receipt system as a way to save paper, and communicate with customers after each purchase. Having digital receipts is a clear way to show customers which products they buy are sustainable and how they gain rewards from those.

Again, we not only wanted to just reflect to the customer, but also begin to change customer behavior. We implemented sustainable alternatives for products the customer bought that were not sustainable.

SUMMARY

As we know, human health and environmental health are closely inexplicably linked. Providing customers with the ability to see their personal impact on the environment will help encourage further purchases of more sustainable products and, in turn, encourage more climate-conscious consumer choices in the future.
